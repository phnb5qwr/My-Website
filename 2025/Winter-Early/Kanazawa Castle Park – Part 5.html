Kanazawa Castle Park - Part 5: Towards Oyama Shrine

From the snow covered Gyokusen'inmaru Garden where rows of yukitsuri quietly lined the slopes I finally start walking toward Nezumitamon From here my destination is Oyama Shrine Oyama Shrine is inseparable from the Maeda family the lords of Kaga It feels like a shrine that holds the Maeda story itself

The wide brown stone steps leading down toward Nezumitamon still hold the morning snowfall on the right half of each tread Beyond Nezumitamon the Nezumitamon Bridge stretches straight ahead its wooden railings fitted with clear acrylic wind panels On both sides of the bridge a walkway has been neatly maintained while only the center line remains covered in snow In front of me an evergreen forest rises in a dense wall of green This is it I think as I move my feet forward

It is early afternoon almost evening and there are hardly any people around After all since morning the city has been battered by strong winds and slanting snow at forty five degrees so this quietness feels natural I walk on under a sky where the snow starts and stops over and over holding my umbrella and listening to the sound of my own footsteps At the far end of Nezumitamon Bridge a stairway descends to the left and at the bottom of those steps lies Oyama Shrine

The first thing that catches my eye is a small beautifully composed shrine garden By the edge of the pond stands a single black pine and at its feet winter reddened sugi moss spreads out like a muted crimson carpet different from the surrounding green moss Behind it a small stone bridge with three arched spans crosses the water as if it were a fragment cut out from a painting For a moment I cannot tell whether I am looking at a real scene or a framed picture A quiet self contained garden opens up before me

Next I notice the statue called Mother and Child Riding the Favorable Wind A mother carries her child on her back and the baby seems to float above the cloth bundle on the wind as if lifted by an invisible current It is a strange yet gentle statue a figure that embodies the image of moving into the future with a tailwind Nearby the Kiku zakura cherry tree still holds its winter branches but a signboard explaining its history stands in front of it showing how carefully it is cherished

Further on a bronze equestrian statue of Maeda Toshiie rises into view On his back he bears a great billowing horo and in his hand he grips a single sided kama spear The peculiar shape of the spearhead looks almost like an inverted cross quietly carrying the atmosphere of the Sengoku era into this snowy garden After paying my respects I turn to face the front of the shrine and see the stained glass like gyaman windows set into the uppermost tier of the main gate roof softly glowing under the winter sky This was what I had wanted to see I realize and a small smile escapes me With that feeling the day's journey comes to an end

Tomorrow I will begin again from this very place I leave the grounds thinking that I have walked a long way today

金沢城公園 - Part 5 尾山神社へ

数々の雪吊りが並ぶ雪景色の美しい玉泉院丸庭園からいよいよ鼠多門へと向かう そうここから向かう先は尾山神社 尾山神社といえば前田家加賀藩 まさに前田家の物語そのもののような神社である

鼠多門へと降りていく広くて茶色い石造りの階段の右半分には今朝からの積雪が残っている 鼠多門を抜けた先には鼠多門橋がまっすぐに架かっており木製の高欄には透明のアクリル防風板が取り付けられている 橋の左右は歩道が整備されていて真ん中にだけ雪が積もっている 正面には常緑の森が静かに広がっている いよいよだ そう思いながら歩を進める

とはいえ昼過ぎ夕方前といった時間帯だったので人通りはほとんどない そもそも朝から強風と斜め四十五度の雪に見舞われた日だ そんなことを思いながら雪が降ったりやんだりする中傘を持って歩いていく 鼠多門橋を渡った先に左へ降りる階段がありその先が尾山神社である

まず目に入ったのはこじんまりと美しくまとまった神苑だった 池のほとりには一本の黒松が立ちその足元には冬の寒さで赤く色づいたスギゴケが広がっている 緑の苔とは違う赤い絨毯のような色合いが雪景色の中でひときわ目を引く その奥には三つのアーチが連なる小さな石の太鼓橋がありまるで絵画の一場面を切り取ったような静けさが漂っていた

次に目に入ったのが母子順風像だった 母が子を背に負いその背の風呂敷の上で赤子が風に乗って浮かび上がるように表現されている 順風に乗って未来へ進む姿を象徴した不思議で温かい像である 菊桜はまだ冬の枝をしていたが手前には由緒を記した看板が置かれ大切に守られていることが伝わってくる

そしてその先に馬に乗った前田利家公の青銅像が立っていた 背には大きく膨らんだ母衣を負い手には片鎌槍を携えている 槍先の独特な形が逆さ十字のように見え戦国の気配を静かに伝えていた 参拝を終えて振り返ると神門最上層の楼閣に施されたギヤマン窓が雪空の下で淡く光り思わず笑みがこぼれた そうこれを見たかった そんな思いの中当日の旅は終わる

明日は再びここから歩き始める それにしても今日はずいぶん歩いたなと思いながらこの場を後にした
